Get Involved

Moose was adopted from the Capital Area Humane Society, which recently changed names to Columbus Humane. This is a great non-profit organization founded in 1957 whose mission statement is: "To fight animal cruelty, help animals in need, and advocate for their well-being." They care for and find homes for cats, dogs, rabbits, ferrets, and more. If you want to help, follow the link to donate time or money to help animals.

You could get trained to be a dog-walker if you want some animal interaction but not actually adopt. Further, it's a great place to adopt from because of the high level of cleanliness of the facility and professionalism of the staff. Cats are kept in individual cages, and visitors must wash their hands between interacting with individual animals. This means the cats are healthy and not ravaged by upper respitory infections and feline herpes the way they sometimes are at no-kill shelters where overcrowded cats mill around together in stressful and germy environments run by hippies.
